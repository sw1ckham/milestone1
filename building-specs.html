Building Specs

Manufactured by skilled craftsmen using premium quality materials, the garden room provides an ideal sheltered and warm environment to relax and work in. There are superb quality joiner made opening double glazed windows (all supplied with lock and key) allowing the sunlight to pour into the building and illuminate the spacious interior. With the inclusion of heavy duty roof shingles, generous roof overhang and professional draft seals fitted to all doors and windows you can rest assured that the garden room is safely protected from the elements.

BEWARE OF CHEAP IMITATIONS

  • The fully insulated garden room features high-grade 44mm interlocking planned and finished scandinavian redwood logs – beware of garden buildings that feature rough edged boards machined from poor quality timber.
  • Features a specially designed 4-way chamfered notch-joint system ensuring a tight fit to all boards with no room for damp or wind penetration – beware of log cabins that feature square cut logs with square notch-joint, often leaving a sloppy joint with large visible gaps.
  • Features factory fitted pressure treated weatherproof heavy duty floor joists – beware of log cabins with untreated floor joists which offer no protection against damp penetration at floor level or worse still cabins that come with no floor joists at all.
  • Features heavy duty premium shingle roofing as standard – beware of log cabins that come with no roof covering or cheap felt, leaving the log cabin completely open to the elements.
  • Features oversized extra large doors and windows – most competitor log cabins use narrow doors which can prove difficult for access.
  • Features key-lockable opening windows and all doors and windows are professionally draft sealed – beware of log cabins with cheap, ill-fitting doors and windows that cannot be properly secured and are susceptible to the weather.
  • Features high quality glazing which is factory silliconed and internally beaded to all doors and windows – beware of log cabins that feature lesser quality materials and provide no window beading.
  • Features a generous all round roof overhang which protects the building from severe conditions – beware of log cabins with little or no roof overhanging leaving them vulnerable to the wind and rain.

PLANNING PERMISSION

Under regulations that came into effect on 1 October 2008 outbuildings are considered to be permitted development, not needing planning permission, subject to the following limits and conditions:

  • No more than half the area of land around the “original house” would be covered by additions or other buildings.
  • No outbuilding on land forward of a wall forming the principal elevation.
  • Outbuildings to be single storey.
  • Maximum eaves height of 2.5mtrs.
  • Maximum overall height of 4 mtrs for dual pitched roof.
  • Maximum overall height of 2.5mtrs within 2 metres of a boundary.
  • With-in the curtilage of listed buildings any outbuilding will require planning permission.

BUILDING REGULATIONS

  • If you want to put up small detached buildings, building regulations will not normally apply if the floor area of the building is less than 15 square metres.
